JSoT CozyConf 2017

Ah, February.

The holidays will be over, summer conference season will still be far off, and, chances are, you'll be trapped at home by ice or snow.

Why not make some hot chocolate, fire up your laptop, and hang out virtually with a bunch of people who geek out about JavaScript and hardware just as much as you?

Introducing the first JavaScript on Things CozyConf, a winter remote conference.

  • Who: People from all over the world who are working on bringing JavaScript to the Internet of Things, whether with johnny-five, Espruino, Kinoma, or something else.
  • What: A chance to get a "state of the union" on JSoT endeavors, learn new tricks, and attend some neat workshops for a variety of skill levels.
  • Where: The comfort of your own home or coffee shop (you can even wear your PJs if you want to). You could also get a group together to stream it in one place.
  • When: February 2017, exact date(s) coming soon.
  • Why: Because the JSoT community is still small enough for something like this to work. And because traveling with hardware demos can be a pain and requires time away from work and family.
  • Cost: To be determined, but the goal is to make this accessible to a wide variety of people. Also, in the spirit of groups like DonutJS, I'd like to donate everything after expenses to a non-profit promoting diversity and inclusiveness in tech.

We need your help!

Since this is the first time this is happening, we need lots of help, such as:

  • Planning: If you've got event planning experience, especially with remote events, get in here.
  • Sponsors: We'll need sponsors to help us pay for things like bandwidth, hosting, and more.
  • Design Skills for Conference Site and Identity: We could use a talented artist or two to help make things look snazzy.
  • Logistics & Technology: Streaming video, audio, and chat for the event itself.
  • Speakers & Workshops: A CFP will be announced soon! We'll need lecture/keynote style updates on major endeavors and workshops across skill levels.
  • Inclusivity & Accessibility: Even though this is a remote conference, we want to strive to make it accessible to as many people as possible. If you're an expert at that, please jump in.
  • Pizzazz: Just because it's remote doesn't mean it can't be awesome. Events like XOXO, Cascadia, and DinosaurJS have really raised the bar. Let's do something creative to get folks excited.

If any of this is up your alley, check out the GitHub issues or hit me up on Twitter and I'll get you plugged in.
